### Unit 2: Political Institutions

#### 2.1: Comparing Parliamentary, Presidential, and Semi-Presidential Systems

- Parliamentary: executive accountable to parliament; head of state ceremonial; vote of no confidence; examples: UK, India, Japan
- Presidential: president elected; head of state + government; separate co-equal legislature; impeachment; examples: US, Brazil, Mexico
- Semi-presidential: president + prime minister share power; examples: France, Russia, South Korea

##### Checks and balances in parliamentary system (as listed)
- Executive checks: vote of no confidence; question period
- Legislative checks: passing laws limiting executive; committees investigate executive
- Judicial checks: judicial review

#### 2.2: Executive Systems

- Executive systems as described (planning/decision-making/goal-directed behavior) + components
- Working memory; inhibitory control; cognitive flexibility; planning/goal-setting

##### Structure/function of executive branch — core course countries (as given)
- UK: monarch ceremonial; prime minister leads government/cabinet/policy
- Russia: president head of state; PM head of government
- China: president head of state; premier oversees civil service
- Iran: supreme leader head of state; president head of government/civil service/foreign policy (per outline)
- Nigeria & Mexico: presidential; president head of state/government; commander in chief; leads cabinet/agencies

#### 2.3: Executive Term Limits

- Russia: two consecutive 6-year terms (with 2020 reset described)
- UK: no formal PM term limits; elections every five years; resignation norm after no-confidence loss
- China: two consecutive 5-year terms (term limits removed for president in 2018)
- Iran: president max two consecutive 4-year terms; supreme leader no term limits
- Mexico: single 6-year term, no re-election
- Nigeria: max two consecutive 4-year terms
- Advantages/disadvantages (as listed)

#### 2.4: Legislative Systems

- Types: unicameral, bicameral, federal, unitary
- Functions: lawmaking, representation, oversight, budgetary control
- UK: Commons + Lords; monarch ceremonial functions
- Russia: Duma + Federation Council; president veto/dissolve; committees; transparency critique
- Iran: Majlis + Guardian Council review for Islamic/constitutional compatibility
- Mexico: Senate + Chamber of Deputies; committees; presidential signature/veto override
- China: NPC + Standing Committee; state council; judiciary (as described)
- Nigeria: Senate + House; bill readings; veto override
